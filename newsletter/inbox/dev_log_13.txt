"I suggest you study for 6 hours a day."

The recruiter from Meta told me after hearing I just got moved forward for a coding interview.

"Oh okay." I said completely dumbfounded at the idea of studying that long.

Being unemployed with 2 young kids and now an interview with Meta it felt like I didn't have much of a choice.

Except I didn't even know how to study for 1 hour.

Don't get me wrong I love to learn and expanding my horizons but that's not what studying for a Meta test is.

Studying for a Meta is sitting down and going through algorithm questions.

Which I do have some interest in how algorithms work as a developer but not 6 hours a day worth.

I'd buckle in to study only to feel like I was wading through waist-deep mud, barely able to drag myself forward.

Which is not a conducive environment for learning.

So not only was it feeling horrible while I was doing it but I was less effective at learning it. All while guilt and shame were building up from what felt like me failing to provide for my family.

"Why can't I just sit down and do this?" constantly reverberating through my head.

Looking back now, it's clear I lacked a way to address the emotional weight studying carried.

That is why I'm making emotion such a core aspect of my game.

If you don't overcome the emotional aspect of a quest you risk it putting you into the emotional hole where motivation and progress goes to die.

I'm making this a core aspect of quest progression.

Now when going to complete a quest like studying for example I can look at it and admit to myself "There is no way I'm going to be able to study for 6 hours, that scares the shit out of me. I can start with 1 hour for today and that will be a win."

While understanding the emotional cost of the quest at hand.

"Honestly studying is just really overwhelming for me because there is no clear problem to solve and feels like it will last forever."

A combination of undiagnosed ADHD and a lifelong struggle with studying really ramped up the emotional toll.

Every quest carries different emotional weight for different people.

Thus making every single emotional toll paid completely unique to us, something only we can understand.

Meaning we need a way to evaluate each quests emotional cost.

A framework of questions created specifically to help you do just that by connecting with your internal emotional radar.

The science is clear.

Understanding and naming an emotion you feel for an upcoming quest makes it easier to start.

Simply saying "The dishes give me anxiety" is enough to reduce resistance.

That is how powerful the knowledge of what you are feeling is, there's a scientifically proven reduction in the brain's fear center response.

Making the quest feel more approachable and much less intimidating.

Here's how it works. Each guild asks a specific question, building on the last, until you understand the real emotional cost:

    Observers:
        Q: What do you feel when you think about studying?
        A: "I feel a deep sense of dread at the prospect of studying."
    Explorers:
        Q: What makes you feel that way?
        A: "It feels like studying will never end and there is no clear point of achievement, it just goes on forever."
    Healers:
        Q: How could you validate and explain that feeling?
        A: "It's okay that it feels hard to study, it's something that has always been intimidating to you."
    Guides:
        Q: What boundary could you set to make this not feel so bad?
        A: "6 hours of study gives me more anxiety than I can afford, I'm going to start with 1."
    Bards:
        Q: How could you make studying not feel quite so bad?
        A: "I'm going to listen to metal while I study and find ways to apply what I'm learning to real world scenarios."

Can you feel the clarity?

Now imagine what you can do by understanding this, simply by understanding you are already better equipped to tackle that quest but then pair that with the idea of planning a restorative quest (like a walk) afterward to help you rebuild those emotional reserves.

This will give you the tools to maintain your emotional bearings throughout the day while still accomplishing those quests you want off your plate.

Emotions happen either way, might as well understand them.

What's a quest that has been draining your emotional reserves and how could these questions help you correct it?

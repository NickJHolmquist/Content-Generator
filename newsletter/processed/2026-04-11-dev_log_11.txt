You know that feeling of coming home from vacation?
That feel good moment where you get to set down the weight of travel and settle back into your home.
After a long drive with two kids and 3 days out of town needless to say I was very ready...
Instead upon opening the door we were greeted with a smell so bad that my wife thought one of our cats had died.
We rushed the kids out onto the back deck and began our investigation.
After opening windows and a quick health check on the cats we realized the smell was coming from under the kitchen sink.
I quickly assumed the worst, the overwhelming stench put me right into solution mode. I called up my parents to see if they could watch the kids while I grabbed my tools and prepared to cut out the floor of the cabinet to track down what had to be a massive mold spot.
Instead my wife insisted that we pause and observe more carefully under the sink.
Only to discover that the small pile of lentils left at the bottom of our garbage can had transmuted into a fly and stench factory somehow strong enough to stink up the whole house.
A discovery that prevented the destruction of our cabinate floor.
The ability to slow down and notice (what I call the Observer's Awareness) doesn't only apply to moldy lentils but to every single aspect of life which when practiced becomes your Resistance Protocol...
Simply start with the trigger of when something doesn't feel right, then pause.
Notice what's actually happening, including how you feel about the situation you're facing. Then apply the right guild skill to better understand the problem and yourself until the solution is obvious.
I initially created the guilds as a means of connecting more deeply with my kids.
Over time that understanding has evolved and now I see that the guilds are about solving problems in real-time.
All types of problems are easier to solve once better understood.
Even the simple understanding of why something doesn't feel quite right can help you move forward.
The Observers guild is how you begin to notice when things aren't going well.
They teach you to ask things like: 'What am I actually seeing? What's the evidence? What am I assuming? How does all of this make me feel?' These questions turned a cabinet-destroying panic into a moment of disbelief at the rotting power of lentils with the simple fix of taking out the trash.
The sooner you pick up on a problem, the sooner you can start collecting data.
With more data, you ask more informed questions (Explorer's Guild) while staying attuned to how you're feeling (Observer's Guild).
If my wife and I hadn't noticed the lentils I would have inadvertently created a new problem.
The same thing is true with creative projects.
It only takes a single revelation to completely change your direction and lead you down a path that feels right.
The Resistance Protocol gives you a mental framework of problem solving.
A way to systematically collect observations through practiced awareness of the situation at hand and how that situation is impacting your emotional state.
Sometimes we set off on what feels like our exact path forward, only to realize after starting down the path that something was off...
Before I had switched to this dev log newsletter structure I had been writing 'Dads Reclamation Quest' and I could tell something was off for months before the right observation led me to a realization...
"This isn't my long term goal, I want to be building a board game."
Observations turn discomfort into data.
Data turns into progress.
What's a problem in your life that could use some fresh observations?

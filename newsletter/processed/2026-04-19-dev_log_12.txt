Life has a tendency to assign you unwanted and required quests.

The type of quests that don't really have a reward but carry a heavy cost when not completed.

File your taxes, call the bank, schedule your dentist appointment, etc...

You know the type, always rattling around in your head and randomly returning to consciousness only to mentally take you away from something else your trying to get done.

Usually this is just annoying but other times it can feel downright cruel.

Those moments where you're already struggling with the weight of what you need to get done when your mind decides to bombard you with the slew of other things you have yet to schedule into your life.

The old me would find an escape hatch, a game, a movie...

Anything to avoid facing down that swell of negative emotions with shame, overwhelm and self doubt becoming unbearably potent.

Now I've found another way.

After years of self work I've seen these escapes for what they are, the very core of the emotions you're trying to avoid.

But recognition is only part of the battle, now you have to deal with the quests.

My big epiphany this week was the need for a place on my board to capture these new quests as they arrive, a living breathing collection of quests.

After so many years spent gaming you'd think I would have recognized this sooner.

Now when those random quests pop up in life I write them on an index card and place it into my quest collector.

This paired with my weekly planning has created a closed loop of productivity.

I am both creating the quests I need to move my creative goals forward while capturing the quests that life assigns me.

Now when I plan, I have all my potential quests for the week ready to be prioritized.

If that sounds like a lot to go through, it is.

Way too much to fit into a week which becomes clear as soon as its all out in front of me, which is a huge improvement over simply feeling like I'm failing to do enough.

I can now make that conscious decision of what I need/really want to get done.

This is giving me the power to MAKE room in my life for the quests I desperately want to keep moving forward, while still ensuring that the important life quests are kept up with each week.

I know what you might be thinking... "Well that doesn't actually help me do things."

And I hear you, I've got some ideas on how to approach our quests with an emotional understanding of what it costs you to accomplish it.

What does emotion have to do with it?

Quests each have their own difficulty level and I'm asserting that the difficulty is not actually set by the raw challenge of the quest ahead but rather how we feel about that challenge.

If you hate it, it's going to be hard.

If you like it, it's going to be easier.

Pretty straight forward idea but it is so often overlooked when we hear about quests boiled down into arbitrary blocks of time.

More on that next week.

What would a brain clear of low tier quests mean for you?

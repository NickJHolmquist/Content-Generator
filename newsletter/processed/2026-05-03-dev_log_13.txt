The smell of death slammed into us as we were leaving our house.

I tried to ignore it.

My wife was quick to point it out "Babe I think something died under the house."

And I knew it was true.

I already had explored every other conceivable option, I even found mushrooms under the front deck and unfortunately were quickly disqualified as the smell culprit.

I did what any man would do...

I went to Harbor Freight and bought a cheap yellow jumpsuit to wear while under the house, along with a respirator, to make sure I was fully covered before venturing under the house.

This was one of those quests that the thought of made my skin crawl.

The first step down into the crawl space I ripped a huge hole into the cheap rubber crotch of the suit.

Immediately making me feel vulnerable to what lurked within.

I crawled on my hands and knees in the dirt looking for the last thing I wanted to find.

Having to carefully maneuver around piping I slowly ventured out to explore the underdark.

The first two sections under the house were clear doing nothing to reduce my dread as I approached the final section.

I crested the edge of the dirt bowl where it dipped down in the corner and made eye contact with the empty eye sockets of a skull attached to the rotting corpse of an opossum.

Quest accomplished -yay- except now I needed to get it out of here.

Using an old snow shovel I got within arms reach of the rotting body and carefully slid the shovel underneath the corpse.

The corpse resisted my careful attempt...

Causing me to force it, disturbing all sorts decaying flesh hungry critters.

And now it was time to extract myself and the body from the bowels of the house, dragging the body laden shovel behind me to avoid crawling in a trail of maggots.

After what felt like an eternity I emerged dirty, sweaty, and relieved.

Until the realization dawned on me that I now am on a new quest of body disposal.

So I did the only thing that I could think to do in that emotionally and physically drained moment, I went out into the green belt and buried it.

While wearing the ripped yellow jumpsuit and respirator, I dug a grave.

Potentially terrifying any neighbors that saw me.

And finally I was done, a wave of relief washing over me as the ridiculousness of what I had just gone through began to sink in.

These types of quests unfortunately have a way of making it onto our calendars.

And often are not something you can really get away with ignoring without potentially serious consequences.

To help address the emotional cost of quests I've come up with the quest matrix.

An emotional quest map that helps you evaluate the cost of a quest before getting into it.

As we covered last week the simple understanding of what a quest makes you feel makes it easier to start, so this is an extension on that very principle.

Except now we are mapping that emotional quest onto a monster.

Past me didn't have these tools so instead I avoided it, which in retrospect did nothing except increase the negative emotions surrounding it.

The core aspect of this quest was going to be shitty no matter what.

All I did in delaying it was allow a mountain of guilt and worry surround the quest at hand.

If I had this game I would have been facing down a Undead Ghoul, a creature that can put a face on the anxiety, overwhelm and disgust I felt going into that quest.

To help overcome that I planned on taking a shower immediately after quest completion.

This helps wash away any physical grime that got on me in addition to making me feel clean after doing such a dirty job while reducing some of the lingering anxiety that arose from venturing under the house.

Then finally taking time to watch a movie or hang with my wife to clear away the rest of the lingering emotion.

Now imagine running through this same process for something that specifically sucks for you.

Right away gaining a starting advantage by understanding what you are feeling.

And then mapping that feeling onto a monster that you can lay out what you need to do to defeat them by counteracting the negative emotions they represent with restorative quests.

Not to mention having an external enemy to move those negative feelings onto to reduce their internal weight.

The goal is to both understand your limits and to respect them.

Sometimes that means pushing through and defeating that monster even when you don't want to and following it with something restorative to rejuvenate.

Other times it means holding off the battle until you have the resources available to tackle it.

Above all else the goal is to make progress.

Sometimes it can take multiple tries to overcome a particularly difficult monster which is okay, every attempt leads to a deeper understanding of your opponent.

The goal is not completion, it is forward momentum in a way that feels good.

What quest monster are you facing and what restorative task could you use to defeat them?

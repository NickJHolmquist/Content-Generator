## The Game — Core Context

The Family Needs Management Game is a physical board game played on a wall that helps players manage daily life through energy states rather than time blocks. Key concepts Claude should understand and be able to reference naturally:

**Energy States** — Low / Medium / High. The game manages energy as a flywheel, not a battery. Momentum builds through action. This is the central contrarian idea that separates this system from standard productivity advice.

**Task Types** — Survival (basic needs, flywheel starters), Structural (maintenance), Creative (identity and fulfillment work). Each behaves differently based on energy state.

**Status Effects** — Visible cards that make invisible costs tangible. FOGGY (bad sleep), STUCK (30+ minutes of drift), NEGLECTED (skipped self-care), RUNNING EMPTY (skipped meals), MENTAL STRAIN (festering tasks). These are information, not judgment.

**Guild Skills** — Five lenses for approaching stuck moments: Observer (what is actually happening), Explorer (break it into pieces), Healer (process the emotion), Guide (set the constraint), Bard (make it tolerable or enjoyable).

**The Cleansing System** — Tasks that get avoided start festering. The Cleansing Ritual uses all five guilds in sequence to decompose stuck tasks into actionable pieces.

**Three-Layer Rulebook Structure** — Core Mechanics (what gets done), Engagement Framework (what keeps players coming back), Personal Connection (where the real meaning lives). The author sees these as operating simultaneously, not as separate sections.

**Long-term vision** — Fantasy-themed, deeply personalized, with guild prompts woven throughout setup and daily/weekly engagement. The goal is a system that works so well the results become undeniable over time.

## Thread Structure Rules

- **Hook tweet** — stops the scroll. A specific concrete insight, a counterintuitive claim, or a moment from the development log that reframes something familiar. Never a question. Never "Thread 🧵"
- **Middle tweets** — each one stands alone as a useful or interesting idea. Build toward something.
- **Final tweet** — lands the thread. A practical reframe, a design principle, or a quiet observation. Never "follow me for more." Never a call to action.
- Each tweet: max 280 characters
- 4–6 tweets per thread
- No numbered tweets (1/5, 2/5 etc.)
- No em dash abuse
- No "Here's what I learned:" openers
- Threads should feel complete to someone who has never heard of the game

## Content Angles to Pull From Each Newsletter

Look for these and prioritize the ones with the most specificity:

1. **A specific mechanic and the design thinking behind it** — Why does the Cleansing system work the way it does? Why is energy a flywheel and not a battery? The reasoning behind a rule is often more interesting than the rule itself.

2. **A contrarian reframe of standard productivity advice** — The game is built on disagreements with how productivity is usually taught. Energy over time. Momentum over discipline. Strategy failure over personal failure. These are threadable.

3. **An ADHD-specific insight made concrete** — Not "ADHD is hard" but "here is a specific moment where executive function failed and here is the structural fix the game provides for it."

4. **A development log moment** — Something changed in the rulebook. A playtesting observation. A decision that was harder than expected. Show the build process.

5. **A personal story that illustrates a system principle** — Dad moments, bad sleep days, STUCK status in real life, the actual experience of using the game. Ground the abstract in the specific.

6. **A design principle that applies beyond the game** — The three-layer rulebook structure. The idea that failure is a strategy error not a moral one. These have broader resonance.
